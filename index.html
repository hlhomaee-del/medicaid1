Concept 1 — What Is Medicare?

Federal · Age-Based · NOT Income-Based

  • Federal program administered by CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services)
  • For people age 65+ and certain qualifying disabilities
  • NOT income-based — that's Medicaid
  • Divided into 4 parts, each with distinct billing implications
  • Covered entities include: healthcare providers, health plans, clearinghouses

⚠️ Exam Trap — Inpatient vs Observation

Admitted ≠ "In the Hospital"

  • Formally ADMITTED → Part A (inpatient)
  • Observation status (in hospital but NOT admitted) → Part B (outpatient)
  • This changes patient responsibility significantly
  • Billers must verify admission status — never assume hospital = Part A

Concept 3 — Medicaid

Joint Federal-State · Income-Based · Payer of Last Resort

  • Joint federal-state program — each state administers its own
  • Based on income — NOT age (that's Medicare)
  • Payer of last resort — always billed LAST after all other insurance
  • Eligibility can change month to month — verify at every visit
  • Lowest reimbursement rates of any payer

Concept 4 — Medigap

Private · Supplements Original Medicare · Fills Cost-Sharing Gaps

  • Medigap is private insurance — sold by private companies
  • Supplements Original Medicare only — NOT Medicare Advantage
  • Fills gaps like the 20% Part B coinsurance
  • Billing: Bill Medicare → receive EOB → bill Medigap as secondary
  • Part C ≠ Medigap. Part C replaces. Medigap supplements. Different workflows.

Concept 5 — TRICARE

Department of Defense · Military Beneficiaries

  • For active duty military, retirees, and families
  • Administered by the Department of Defense
  • Plan types: TRICARE Prime (HMO-like) · TRICARE Select (PPO-like) · TRICARE for Life (retirees — Medicare primary, TRICARE secondary)
  • Do not assume civilian plan rules apply — verify the specific plan
